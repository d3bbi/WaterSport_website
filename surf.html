WINDSURF

"I can't change the direction of the wind, but I can adjust my sails to always reach my destination."Jimmy Dean

Why practice windsurf in Malta?

Malta is the ideal place for windsurfing due to the warm waters and the easy accessibility of windsurfing spots. Windsurfing is a form of sailing, whereby you stand on a board attached to a sail and you move across the water, through the force of the wind. On the other hand surfing depends on the waves, whereby you stand on a surfing board and ride the waves. The best time to go windsurfing in Malta is between November and March, when the wind conditions are the best.

Ghallis Rocks

Ghallis Rock is the most popular wave windsurfing spot in Malta when the wind is from West/NortWest/North. Ghallis Rock is situated along the coast road which connects Sliema with the north of the island. The spot is is usually pretty safe however the foreshore is rocky and beginner must be cautious in the shore-break. Intermediate wind surfers can surf the break off the point formed by the rock while more experienced surfers can sail the windward side of the rock.
